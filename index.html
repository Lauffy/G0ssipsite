Gossip. What is it?

Gossip is casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about other people, typically involving details that are not confirmed as being true.Gossiping is used to give humans the ability to spread valuable information to very large social networks, as a means of bonding


Gossip vs. Rumors

Sometimes gossip and rumors is used synonymously, when actually they're not the same thing. Here are some differences

Rumors

  • Unverified pieces of information
  • Often involves speculation
  • Unknown if information is true
  • May change slightly as retold
  • Information usually not harmful to another person

Gossip

  • Juicy or scandalous story
  • Hurtful for another person
  • Unknown if information is true
  • Usually involves things not discussed publicly
  • May humiliate the person it's about










Impact of Both

Gossip and rumors can destroy a person’s self-confidence and affect their self-esteem.It also can lead to depression, suicidal thoughts, eating disorders, anxiety, and a host of other issues.Gossip and rumors can alienate friends, ruin reputations, and even lead to ostracizing behavior and other forms of relational aggression.


Fun Facts

  • Gossip makes up 55% of our conversations
  • Gossip can be good,bad, or neutral
  • Females gossip more than males
  • On average, humans gossip 52 minutes each day
  • Extroverts gossip significantly more than introverts
